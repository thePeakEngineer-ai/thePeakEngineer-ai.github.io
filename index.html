Top Beginner-Friendly Trails

Perfect trails for those new to hiking in the Seattle area

Discovery Park

Seattle's largest park with stunning views of Puget Sound

  • 15 min from Seattle
  • 470 ft elevation gain
  • 4.4 miles loop
  • 1.5-2.0 hours

Snoqualmie Falls

Easy trail leading to one of Washington's most famous waterfalls

  • 35 min from Seattle
  • 250 ft elevation gain
  • 1.4 miles out & back
  • 45-60 minutes

Gold Creek Pond Loop

Scenic alpine lake with mountain views

  • 1 hour from Seattle
  • 45 ft elevation gain
  • 1.1 miles loop
  • 30-45 minutes

Franklin Falls

Family-friendly trail to a beautiful waterfall

  • 50 min from Seattle
  • 400 ft elevation gain
  • 2.0 miles out & back
  • 1-1.5 hours

Heybrook Lookout

Historic fire lookout with panoramic views

  • 1 hour 15 min from Seattle
  • 850 ft elevation gain
  • 1.9 miles out & back
  • 1.5-2 hours

Top Intermediate Trails

Challenging yet rewarding trails for experienced hikers

Skyline Trail (Mt. Rainier)

Stunning alpine meadows with panoramic views of Mt. Rainier

  • 2.5 hours from Seattle
  • 1,700 ft elevation gain
  • 5.5 miles loop
  • 3-4 hours

Poo Poo Point

Popular trail with paragliding launch point and city views

  • 30 min from Seattle
  • 1,800 ft elevation gain
  • 6.6 miles out & back
  • 3-4 hours

Rattlesnake Ledge

Scenic overlook of Rattlesnake Lake and surrounding mountains

  • 40 min from Seattle
  • 1,430 ft elevation gain
  • 5.3 miles out & back
  • 2-3 hours

Lake 22

Beautiful alpine lake surrounded by mountain peaks

  • 1 hour 15 min from Seattle
  • 1,450 ft elevation gain
  • 5.9 miles out & back
  • 3-4 hours

Snow Lake

Crystal clear alpine lake with stunning mountain backdrop

  • 1 hour from Seattle
  • 1,800 ft elevation gain
  • 6.7 miles out & back
  • 3-4 hours

Top Difficult Trails

Advanced trails for experienced hikers seeking challenging adventures

Maple Pass Loop

Spectacular alpine loop with panoramic views of the North Cascades

  • 3 hours from Seattle
  • 2,200 ft elevation gain
  • 6.5 miles loop
  • 4-6 hours

Lake Ingalls

High alpine lake surrounded by jagged peaks and mountain goats

  • 2.5 hours from Seattle
  • 2,800 ft elevation gain
  • 8.9 miles out & back
  • 6-8 hours

Gothic Basin

Rugged trail to a stunning alpine basin with turquoise lakes

  • 2 hours from Seattle
  • 3,100 ft elevation gain
  • 9.3 miles out & back
  • 6-8 hours

Cascade Pass

Iconic North Cascades trail with spectacular mountain vistas (can be short or long)

  • 3.5 hours from Seattle
  • 1,800-3,900 ft elevation gain
  • 6.9 - 11.6 miles out & back
  • 4-8 hours

Colchuck Lake

Stunning alpine lake with crystal clear waters and dramatic peaks

  • 2.5 hours from Seattle
  • 2,300 ft elevation gain
  • 8.7 miles out & back
  • 5-7 hours
